## 5. Reading the results

- **Smart Persistence is the bar to beat.** On clear-sky-dominated `uk_pv` it is very strong: the trivial Persistence, Hourly-climatology and Seasonal-naive references are all *worse* (SS ≈ −0.41…−0.51), confirming the Skill-Score denominator is well chosen and there is no trivial-seasonality leak.
- **Three models clear the bar with significance:** MLP (SS +0.10), LightGBM (+0.085) and TFT-lite (+0.049) beat Smart Persistence with DM p<0.05 *and* a bootstrap CI excluding 0, and win on 86–93 % of test plants. iTransformer is marginally positive (+0.013, significant but small); DLinear and PatchTST are *below* Smart Persistence — the embarrassingly-simple-linear and patch-transformer checks both underperform here, which is the expected "covariates + nonlinearity matter, raw patching does not" story for short-horizon PV.
- **Per-horizon:** all learned models beat Smart Persistence from h1 onward and the gap widens with lead time (SP flattens at ≈0.085 NMAE by h5 while LightGBM/TFT stay ≈0.074). Only the first 5 of 12 horizon steps carry daylight in this non-overlapping eval tiling (≈4.9 valid future steps/window); night positions are masked to 0 for every model identically.
- **CRPS** is only defined for the quantile-native models (LightGBM, TFT-lite): 0.0561 vs 0.0577. Point models show `—` by protocol (§4.3).

## 6. Caveats & local-compute deviations

These runs were produced on a single MacBook Air M2 (16 GB) before the consolidated `thesis-dataset` was assembled and while `goes_pvdaq` was still downloading, so they are the **numerical-track `uk_pv` slice only**; cross-dataset (S3), LOPO `goes_pvdaq`, and the multimodal tiers are not included. `goes_pvdaq` is now fully available in `thesis-dataset/dataset_all.parquet` (10 plants, 104,792 rows, 256px RGB frames in `images_all.h5`), so S3/LOPO/multimodal-`goes` are now unblocked (pending a cluster run on the dataset of record — DATASET_CONTRACT.md §1.0).

1. **Tier-2 training stride.** The protocol default is `--train-stride 1` (980 k overlapping train windows). To keep 5 models × 3 seeds tractable on CPU/MPS, tier-2 models were trained with `--train-stride 12` (non-overlapping history → 49.9 k windows). LightGBM and all eval windows are unchanged (`--eval-stride 12`, the full 10 129-window test set). This subsampling plausibly handicaps the data-hungry transformers (PatchTST/iTransformer) more than MLP/DLinear; treat the tier-2 transformer rows as a lower bound pending a full-stride cluster run.
2. **Seeds.** Trained models use seeds {42, 43, 44} (mean ± std shown). Tier-0/reference models are deterministic single runs. Std bands are tight (≤0.013 SS), so ranking is stable.
3. **Significance** uses the seed-42 per-window loss sidecar for trained models (the `*_agg` files store scalar means only). DM uses the masked-MAE differential, so it agrees with the NMAE column in sign.
4. **Not yet populated:** efficiency table (§4.6 params/latency/VRAM), Tier-3 TSFMs (Chronos-2/TimesFM/TiRex — `--group tier3`, run on the cluster), Tier-4 adaptation, and the vision tiers. The headline table slots are ready for them.
